# AviationIQ - Product Requirements Document
**Version 2.0 | March 2026 | Confidential**

---

## 1. Executive Summary

AviationIQ is a cloud-based SaaS platform designed to digitize and streamline ground handling operations for regional airlines in Canada and the United States. The platform replaces fragmented paper-based processes, Excel spreadsheets, and informal communication channels with a unified, real-time operations system accessible from desktop and mobile.

The ground handling industry, particularly among regional carriers, continues to rely on manual workflows that create operational inefficiencies, compliance risks, and poor visibility for airline clients. AviationIQ addresses this gap with modular, affordable, and easy-to-adopt tooling built by a practitioner with firsthand ramp operations experience at Toronto Pearson International Airport.

---

## 2. Problem Statement

**What regional airlines are doing today:**
- Paper-based turnaround checklists filled out manually on the ramp
- Excel spreadsheets for grooming scheduling and time tracking
- Email and WhatsApp for shift handovers and incident reporting
- Phone calls for baggage irregularity coordination with passengers
- Printed forms for aircraft damage documentation
- Manual consolidation of data for SLA reporting to airline clients

**Pain points generated by these processes:**
- Turnaround delays caused by poor visibility of event timing and task ownership
- Grooming inefficiencies due to lack of standardized benchmarks by aircraft type
- Baggage cases unresolved for extended periods due to poor tracking
- Damage disputes with airlines that lack photographic evidence or timestamps
- Critical shift handover information lost between outgoing and incoming supervisors
- SLA compliance reports that take hours to produce manually at month end

**Market opportunity:**
There are over 90 regional airlines operating in the United States and Canada. The majority operate between 10 and 100 daily flights across 3 to 15 stations. Enterprise solutions from SITA, Amadeus, or Sabre are priced for major carriers and are inaccessible to this segment. AviationIQ fills this gap with a right-sized, affordable, and operationally focused platform built specifically for this market.

## 3. Product Vision

> "The all-in-one operations platform for regional airlines. Replace paper, Excel, and WhatsApp with one system built for the ramp."

AviationIQ aims to become the operational backbone of regional airline ground handling in North America, enabling teams to execute flights with greater consistency, accountability, and data visibility.

**Long-term vision:** Evolve into a network platform where airlines can benchmark performance across handlers, and handlers can attract airline clients based on verified operational metrics.

## 6. Module Specifications

### 6.1 Turnaround Operations

The operational core of AviationIQ. Real-time visibility and timestamp capture for every phase of a flight turnaround.

**Flight Board**
Real-time view of all flights for the day with current status, assigned crew, and time to departure. Color-coded by status: On Track, At Risk, Delayed, Completed. The digital equivalent of the supervisor whiteboard. Powered by Supabase Realtime, updates automatically without page refresh.

**Turnaround Tracker**
Sequential event logging per flight. Events captured in order: Aircraft Arrival, Door Open, Deplaning Start, Deplaning End, Cleaning Start, Cleaning End, Catering Confirmed, Fueling Confirmed, Boarding Start, Boarding End, Door Close, Pushback. Each event shows who logged it and at what time. Alerts trigger when an event exceeds its planned window.

**Event Log**
Immutable timestamped record of all turnaround events per flight. Searchable by date, flight number, and station. Fully exportable for audits and dispute resolution.

**Alert Center**
Configurable alert thresholds per event type and per airline client SLA. Delivers in-app and email notifications via Gmail SMTP to the designated supervisor.

---

### 6.2 Grooming Management

Manages cabin cleaning operations with work order automation, agent tracking, and performance benchmarking by aircraft type and service level.

**Aircraft Type Standards**
Configuration table of expected cleaning duration by aircraft type (B737, A320, CRJ200, CRJ900, Q400, etc.) and cleaning level (Transit Clean, Full Clean, Deep Clean). Set once per operator, applied automatically to every work order.

**Work Orders**
Automatic creation of a grooming work order upon aircraft arrival. Includes aircraft type, cleaning level, required number of agents, and standard duration. Supervisor confirms or adjusts before releasing to the team.

**Agent Assignment**
Supervisor assigns named agents to each work order. System records entry time and completion time per agent. Calculates actual duration versus the aircraft type standard automatically.

**Performance Dashboard**
Comparative view of actual vs. standard cleaning time by flight, by agent, by shift, and by month. Highlights recurring bottlenecks and identifies top-performing agents.

---

### 6.3 Damage Reports

Digitizes the aircraft and equipment damage reporting process with mandatory photo capture, multi-level approval, and automatic airline notification via Gmail SMTP.

**New Incident**
Mobile-first form capturing: flight number, aircraft registration, damage location on aircraft diagram, description, responsible agent, minimum one photo required, and GPS-stamped timestamp.

**Approval Workflow**
Sequential approval chain: Agent submits, Supervisor reviews and adds comments, Station Manager gives final approval. Each stage records reviewer name and timestamp. No email goes out until final approval is complete.

**Airline Notification**
Upon final approval, a formatted incident report with all photos and metadata is automatically sent to the affected airline's designated safety contact via Gmail SMTP.

**Incident History**
Searchable log of all incidents filtered by station, date range, airline client, and responsible agent. Used for audits, insurance claims, and operational trend analysis.

---

### 6.4 Baggage Services

End-to-end management of baggage irregularity cases from initial report through delivery confirmation, with automated passenger communication via Gmail SMTP at each status change.

**New Irregularity**
Structured intake form: flight number, bag tag number, passenger name and contact information, issue type (Lost, Damaged, Delayed, Misrouted), photos if applicable.

**Case Tracker**
Status pipeline: Reported, Located, In Transit, Out for Delivery, Delivered, Closed. Each status change is timestamped and attributed to the updating agent.

**Passenger Notifications**
Automatic email to the passenger at each status change via Gmail SMTP. No manual outreach required. Notification templates configurable per airline client. SMS via Twilio added in a future release when the first enterprise client requires it.

**Delivery Log**
Confirmation of delivery with passenger digital acknowledgment. Closes the case and archives the complete history.

---

### 6.5 Workforce Management

Manages agent scheduling, flight assignments, attendance, and shift-to-shift communication.

**Shift Planner**
Definition of recurring shift patterns per station with assigned agents. Handles standard shifts and irregular coverage days.

**Flight Assignment**
Assignment of agents to specific flights by role: Ramp Agent, Wing Walker, Marshaller, Cabin Cleaner, Customer Service Agent. Prevents coverage gaps and double-booking.

**Attendance Log**
Digital check-in and check-out per agent per shift. Records actual hours worked versus scheduled. Input by supervisor or agent self-service on mobile.

**Shift Handover**
Structured digital report completed by the outgoing supervisor: flights completed, open incidents, equipment issues, and notes for the incoming team. Delivered in-app and by email via Gmail SMTP automatically at shift end.

---

### 6.6 Airline Client Portal

Read-only access portal for airline clients to monitor the performance of their handled flights. Eliminates manual monthly reporting entirely.

**Flight Performance**
Turnaround times for all handled flights. On-time performance rate. Visual comparison against contracted SLA thresholds with red, yellow, and green indicators.

**Incident Reports**
Full history of damage reports related to the airline's aircraft, including photos, approval chain, and resolution status.

**Baggage Summary**
Irregularity count by flight and month, case resolution rate, and average time from report to delivery.

**Monthly Report**
Automated PDF report generated at month end summarizing all KPIs for that airline client. Downloadable directly from the portal. No action required from the handler.

---

### 6.7 Analytics and Reporting

Cross-module analytics for operations leadership providing a consolidated view of performance across all stations and metrics.

**Operations Dashboard**
Executive KPI view: turnaround on-time rate, average grooming time by aircraft type, baggage irregularity rate per 1,000 flights, incident frequency, and SLA compliance score by airline client.

**Custom Reports**
Report builder with filters by date range, station, airline client, and module. Outputs a summary table with key statistics.

**Export Center**
Export any report to PDF or Excel. Reports are branded with the operator logo and formatted for client delivery.

**Trend Analysis**
Month-over-month comparison of all key metrics. Identifies seasonal patterns and measures the operational impact of process changes over time.

---

### 6.8 Settings and Administration

Platform configuration layer. Foundation for all operational modules.

**Organization**
Company name, logo, active stations, and airline client relationships.

**Users**
Role-based access control with five roles: Admin, Station Manager, Supervisor, Agent, and Airline Client (read-only). Invitation-based onboarding by email via Gmail SMTP.

**Aircraft Registry**
Catalog of aircraft types operated with cleaning standards and turnaround benchmarks per type.

**SLA Configuration**
Per-airline-client definition of maximum allowed time for each turnaround event. Feeds the alert system and the client portal performance scoring.

**Notifications**
Configuration of alert recipients, delivery channels (in-app and email via Gmail SMTP in v1.0), and threshold values per event type and per station.

## 9. Technical Architecture

### 9.1 Tech Stack

| Layer | Technology | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Frontend | Next.js 14 App Router, TypeScript | SSR, routing, Server Actions |
| UI | Tailwind CSS, shadcn/ui | Speed of development, mobile-first |
| State | React Query (TanStack) | Server state sync, realtime cache |
| Forms | React Hook Form, Zod | Validation, type safety |
| Backend | Supabase (PostgreSQL, Auth, Storage, Realtime, Edge Functions) | All-in-one, no separate API needed |
| Email | Nodemailer with Gmail SMTP via Google App Password | Free, reliable, zero external dependency |
| Deploy | Vercel | Zero-config, preview deployments, CDN |

### 9.2 Architecture Overview

```
User (Supervisor on mobile, ramp)
        |
        v
   Next.js App (Vercel)
        |
   App Router + Server Components
        |
        |--- Server Actions (mutations)
        |--- React Query (data fetching and cache)
        |--- Supabase Realtime (Flight Board live updates)
        |
        v
   Supabase
        |--- PostgreSQL (multi-tenant with RLS)
        |--- Auth (5 roles, magic links or email/password)
        |--- Storage (damage report photos, generated PDFs)
        |--- Realtime (Flight Board)
        |--- Edge Functions (SLA calculation, PDF generation, email triggers)
        |
        v
   Gmail SMTP via Nodemailer
        |--- Turnaround alerts
        |--- Damage report notifications to airlines
        |--- Baggage case updates to passengers
        |--- Shift handover reports
        |--- User invitations

   Future integrations (Phase 4, enterprise clients only):
        |--- Resend (replaces Gmail SMTP at scale)
        |--- Twilio (SMS notifications for passengers)
        |--- AviationStack API (live flight data)
        |--- Airline PSS integrations via REST API
```

### 9.3 Multi-Tenancy Model

Single PostgreSQL schema with `organization_id` on every table. Row Level Security enforced at the database level so no data from one organization is ever accessible to another, regardless of application-level bugs. This architecture supports up to 500 concurrent customers without schema changes.

## 10. Development Approach: Claude Code Agent Teams

### 10.1 Agent Team Structure

AviationIQ is built using Claude Code Max with Agent Teams enabled, running 3 specialized agents in parallel. This reduces module development time from weeks to days.

**Enable Agent Teams:**
```
CLAUDE_CODE_EXPERIMENTAL_AGENT_TEAMS=1
```

### 10.2 Agent Definitions

**Database Agent**
Owns everything that lives in Supabase and never touches the UI. Responsible for SQL migrations, table design, relationships, RLS policies per role and organization, Edge Functions for business logic, database indexes, and seed data for testing.

**Frontend Agent**
Owns everything the user sees and interacts with. Responsible for Next.js pages, React components, Tailwind styling, shadcn/ui integration, React Hook Form with Zod validation, Server Actions, and React Query for data fetching and caching.

**Integration Agent**
Owns the glue layer between systems. Responsible for Supabase Auth configuration with 5 roles, middleware for route protection, Nodemailer setup with Gmail SMTP and Google App Password, email templates for all notification types, environment variable management for local and Vercel, and deployment pipeline configuration.

### 10.3 Agent Domain Rules (CLAUDE.md)

```
Database Agent owns:
  /supabase/migrations/**
  /supabase/functions/**
  /supabase/seed/**

Frontend Agent owns:
  /app/**
  /components/**
  /lib/validations/**

Integration Agent owns:
  /lib/supabase/**
  /lib/email/**
  /middleware.ts
  /.env.local
  /vercel.json
```

### 10.4 Execution Order

**Sprint 1 (days 1 to 2):** Integration Agent runs first. Sets up the full project base: Next.js, Supabase connection, Auth with 5 roles, middleware, Gmail SMTP config, and initial Vercel deployment. Unblocks the other two agents.

**Sprint 2 (days 3 to 5):** Database Agent and Frontend Agent run in parallel. Database creates the full MVP schema with RLS policies. Frontend builds the Settings module and dashboard layout while waiting for schema.

**Sprint 3 (days 6 to 10):** All three agents run in parallel per module. Database adds Edge Functions and refines RLS. Frontend builds Turnaround Operations, Grooming, and Damage Reports. Integration Agent connects email notifications for each module.

## 14. Assumptions and Risks

**Key assumptions:**
- Regional airlines are willing to pay for a purpose-built tool if onboarding takes less than one week
- Ramp agents can adopt a mobile app with minimal training given simple UX design
- Gmail SMTP with Google App Password is reliable enough for v1.0 notification volume
- Regulatory environment for ground handling data storage is compatible with a cloud-first architecture

**Key risks:**
- Sales cycle longer than expected due to procurement processes at regional carriers
- Resistance from ramp supervisors accustomed to paper-based workflows
- Gmail SMTP daily sending limits (500 emails/day on standard Gmail, 2,000 on Google Workspace) becoming a constraint before Resend is integrated
- Data security requirements from airline clients requiring on-premise deployment options

**Mitigations:**
- Offer a 30-day free trial with guided onboarding to reduce procurement friction
- Design for one-tap interactions on mobile to minimize adoption resistance
- Monitor Gmail sending volume and trigger Resend migration proactively before hitting limits
- Evaluate SOC 2 Type II certification roadmap in Year 1 to address enterprise security requirements
